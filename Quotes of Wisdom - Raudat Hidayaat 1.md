# **Quotes of Wisdom**

### **Extracted from Raudat Hidayaat 1**

Below is the ordered collection of English quotes extracted from the document, matched to their respective page numbers.

| Page Number | Quote   |
| :---: | :---- |
| 15 | The deed dearest to Allah Ta'ala is that which is most consistent, even if small. |
| 16 | When someone distinguished from community visits you, honour him. |
| 17 | Gladly accept Allah Ta'ala's will and you will \[truly\] become a zaahid. *zaahid: one who belittles and voluntarily forsakes worldly pleasures* |
| 18 | Perfect your wudu' and you will live longer. |
| 19 | Completing an act of kindness is better than starting it. If you start an act of kindness, complete it. |
| 20 | Seek help in the fulfillment of \[your\] needs by concealing them. |
| 22 | Do good to the worthy and the unworthy. If a person is worthy, then he is deserving of that good. If a person is unworthy, then you are one who does good regardless. |
| 24 | Know: if all Creation unites in order to provide you with something that Allah Ta'ala does not intend to give you, it will be unable to do so. Likewise, all Creation will be powerless to prevent that which Allah Ta'ala intends reaches you. |
| 25 | Act according to Allah Ta'ala's faraa'id (what He has obligated) and you will \[truly\] become one who worships Him. |
| 26 | The bane of beauty is vanity. *bane: a cause of harm and ruin vanity: excessive pride in or admiration of one's own appearance or achievements* |
| 27 | The bane of generosity is extravagance. |
| 28 | The bane of speech is lying. |
| 29 | The bane of nobility is to be overproud. |
| 30 | The bane of forbearance is foolishness. *forbearance: patient self-control that characterises deliberately holding back from action* |
| 31 | The bane of munificence is recounting favours. *munificence: to be large-hearted and very generous* |
| 32 | The bane of courage is oppression. Courage should not lead one to transgress the limits of what is just and right. |
| 33 | The bane of cleverness is conceitedness. |
| 34 | The bane of 'ibaadat is listlessness. *listlessness: lack of energy and enthusiasm* |
| 35 | The bane of knowledge is to forget. |
| 36 | Enquire about your neighbour before purchasing a house and about your companion before embarking upon a journey. |
| 37 | The dictation of good is better than silence. However, silence is better than the dictation of evil. |
| 38 | What most entitles people to enter jannat is the taqwaa of Allah Ta'ala and good character. |
| 39 | Jealousy consumes hasanaat (good deeds) the way fire consumes wood. |
| 40 | Through the goodness of his character, a man attains the status of one dedicated to rozah and namaaz. |
| 42 | In all His justice and fairness, Allah Ta'ala has determined that comfort and relief shall reside in faith and contentment. Likewise, He has determined that worry and sorrow shall reside in doubt and discontentment. |
| 43 | When the intention \[behind a deed\] is to obtain aakherat, Allah Ta'ala grants dunyaa \[along with aakherat\]. However, when the intention is worldly, He refuses to grant aakherat. |
| 44 | Allah Ta'ala prohibits you from engaging in gossip, squandering (wasting) wealth and questioning too much (either due to excessive inquisitiveness or for material gain). |
| 45 | A small number of deeds undertaken with knowledge and understanding amounts to a lot. However, a large number of deeds carried out in ignorance and without understanding amounts to little. |
| 46 | The person who prohibits halaal is the same as the one who permits haraam. Prohibiting halaal is as sinful as permitting haraam. |
| 47 | It is Sunnat to walk one's guest to the door of the house. *Sunnat: Rasulullah's SA words and acts* |
| 48 | Beware of assumptions, for statements based upon them are most likely to be untruthful. |
| 49 | Barakat lies with your elders. |
| 50 | The cowardly businessman goes unrewarded, while the one who is courageous is rewarded with his livelihood. |
| 52 | In the zill (shade) of the 'arsh (Throne), on the day there is no other zill, will be the man who leaves his home to trade and seek sustenance from Allah Ta'ala's grace. He does this to prevent himself from asking of others and to provide for his family. |
| 53 | Planning is one half of life. Proper planning is essential for a fulfilling life. |
| 54 | Exchange gifts with one another for doing so increases affection and cleanses hearts of grudges and ill will. |
| 56 | Three people remain untouched by the trials and tribulations of this world and the hereafter: the person who offers sincere advice to Muslims, the person who guides towards good, the person who is content with Allah Ta'ala's judgement and will. |
| 57 | Having a sense of shame is goodness in its entirety. |
| 58 | All Creation is a family dependent on Allah Ta'ala. The most beloved of all Creation to Him, therefore, is the one who benefits His family the most. |
| 59 | Du'aa' is a Mumin's weapon. |
| 60 | The person who provides water should be the last to drink. |
| 61 | Greet your family with salaam and the well-being of your home will greatly increase. |
| 62 | When you meet someone from my Ummah, greet him with salaam and your hasanaat will greatly increase. |
| 63 | The worst of all earnings are earnings from interest. |
| 64 | Sadaqah given in secret extinguishes Allah Ta'ala's anger. |
| 65 | The reward of a person who is thankful for what he eats is similar to that of one who endures his rozah. |
| 66 | Seeking knowledge is obligatory upon every Muslim man and woman. |
| 67 | I am amazed by a person who laughs very loudly, and all the while he is unaware of whether he has pleased Allah Ta'ala or angered Him. |
| 68 | I am amazed by a person who desires worldly pleasures and all the while Death seeks him. |
| 70 | Knowledge is a Mumin's companion, Forbearance together with Sagacity is his vizier, Intellect is his guide, Action is his leader, Competence is his father, Kindness is his brother and Endurance is the commander of his armies. |
| 71 | Al-Quran itself is the cure and du'aa' itself is 'ibaadat. |
| 72 | Speak al-Haq (what is true and right) even though it may be bitter. |
| 73 | The betrayal is great when your brother believes you to be truthful about what you tell him \[because it comes from you\], and all along you are lying. |
| 74 | The honour of a home lies in sweeping it (keeping it clean). |
| 75 | The honour of a garment lies in folding it properly. |
| 76 | Do not speak ill of Muslims behind their backs nor pursue their flaws. |
| 77 | Do not sleep unless you are in a state of tahaarat, for if you pass away \[while sleeping in a state of tahaarat\] you will die a shaheed. |
| 78 | Do not make a promise to your brother that you will not keep. |
| 79 | Not a single one of you should ever wish for death as a result of a hardship that has befallen him. |
| 80 | A Muslim is not permitted to sever relations with his brother for more than three days. |
| 82 | A man will not perfect his belief in Allah Ta'ala without five qualities: having faith in Allah Ta'ala, entrusting all matters to Him, submitting to His command, accepting His judgement gladly, enduring His trials. |
| 83 | A Mumin will not be bitten twice in the same snake nest. A Mumin will stay away from what has harmed him once before. |
| 84 | A powerful person is not one who brings another to the ground. Truly powerful is he who controls himself when angry. |
| 85 | A wealthy person is not one who has an abundance of material possessions. Truly wealthy is he who is content with whatever he has and is not dependent on anyone. |
| 86 | A person is not one of us if he does not respect elders, does not show kindness to the young, does not instruct \[himself or others\] towards that which is good, nor does he prevent \[himself or others\] from that which is wrong. |
| 87 | A youth will not show deference to an elder out of consideration for his age, but Allah Ta'ala will provide him with someone who will show him deference when he reaches that age as well. |
| 88 | A man does not open for himself the door that leads to begging, but Allah Ta'ala opens for him the door that leads to poverty. |
| 89 | No act of Allah Ta'ala's taa'at (obedience) is rewarded more swiftly than the act of ensuring good relations with one's family and relatives. |
| 90 | A father has not given his child a better gift than good manners. |
| 92 | A vile companion is like a blacksmith fanning a fire with his bellows: even if he does not burn you with the sparks of his fire, his odour will certainly stay with you. |
| 93 | A virtuous companion is like a merchant of Dari musk: even if he does not offer you his perfume, his fragrance will certainly linger with you. |
| 94 | A Mumin is like a honeybee: it does not consume nor give except that which is pure and wholesome. |
| 95 | He whose advice is sought is considered to be trustworthy. Therefore, it is his responsibility to give reliable, honest advice. |
| 96 | A Muslim is one from whose words and actions other Muslims remain unharmed. |
| 97 | If a person wishes to be the wealthiest of all people, he should rely more on what is in Allah Ta'ala's hands than what is in his own. |
| 98 | If a person builds a masjid for Allah Ta'ala, even if like the nest of a sandgrouse, Allah Ta'ala will build for him a home in jannat. |
| 99 | He who humbles himself before Allah Ta'ala, Allah Ta'ala will raise him. |
| 100 | The merit of a man's Islam is in disregarding matters that do not concern him. |
