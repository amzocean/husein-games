⚓ Battleship

Best Overall Pick — "You sunk my battleship!"

Classic ship placement + guessing grid. Each player places 5 ships on a 10×10 grid, then takes turns firing shots at the opponent's grid. Hit/miss/sunk feedback with progressive deduction narrows down remaining ships.

Fun★★★★★
Strategy★★★★☆
Luck★★★☆☆
Competition★★★★★
⏱ Round: 10-15 min
📝 Estimate: ~800-1000 LOC
🔧 Difficulty: Medium
Why it fits: Perfect strategy/luck balance. Placement phase is strategic, early shots are lucky, mid-game becomes deductive. Very similar Socket.IO architecture to Ludo (turn-based, room codes, game state sync). Ship drag-placement adds a unique pre-game layer none of your current games have.
Key components: 10×10 grid UI (×2), ship drag-and-drop placement, shot/result WebSocket protocol, ship sinking detection, CSS grid + SVG ships with couple theming.
🥈

🔴 Connect Four

Quickest to Build — fast drops, surprise wins

Drop colored discs into a 7-column, 6-row grid. Gravity pulls them to the lowest open slot. First to get 4 in a row (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) wins. Simple rules, satisfying drop animation, and quick rematches.

Fun★★★★☆
Strategy★★★★☆
Luck★★☆☆☆
Competition★★★★☆
⏱ Round: 3-5 min
📝 Estimate: ~500-600 LOC
🔧 Difficulty: Low
Why it fits: Simplest to build of all options. Gravity-based drop mechanic is satisfying. Can add couple-themed tokens (hearts vs stars). Very quick rounds create a "one more game" factor. Could be a weekend project.
⚠ Concern: Less luck than you prefer. Can feel "solved" after many games if both players learn the meta. First-player advantage is significant.
Key components: 7×6 grid with gravity, disc drop animation (CSS transition), 4-in-a-row detection (8 directions), turn sync via Socket.IO, win highlight animation.
🥉

🔲 Dots & Boxes

Hidden Gem — deceptively deep, chain reactions are thrilling

Grid of dots. Take turns drawing one line between two adjacent dots. Complete all 4 sides of a box to claim it + earn an extra turn. Chain reactions let you capture multiple boxes in one turn. Most boxes at the end wins.

Fun★★★★☆
Strategy★★★★★
Luck★★☆☆☆
Competition★★★★★
⏱ Round: 5-10 min
📝 Estimate: ~700-800 LOC
🔧 Difficulty: Medium
Why it fits: Extremely underrated. Looks simple but has real depth. The "extra turn on box completion" mechanic creates exciting chain reactions. Endgames are dramatic with tight scores. Grid-based UI is familiar territory. Easy to theme (heart-shaped boxes?).
⚠ Concern: Low luck factor. Early moves feel neutral until the midgame "sacrifice" decisions kick in. Strategy-heavy players may dominate.
Key components: Dot grid rendering (SVG/Canvas), line click detection (horizontal & vertical edges), box completion check (O(1) per move), chain turn logic, score tracking, Socket.IO sync.

🎲 Yahtzee Duel

Best Strategy/Luck Balance — dice drama every turn

Roll 5 dice, choose which to keep, reroll up to 3 times per turn. Fill one of 13 scoring categories each turn (three-of-a-kind, full house, straight, Yahtzee, etc.). Compete for highest total score. Both players can roll simultaneously — less waiting!

Fun★★★★☆
Strategy★★★☆☆
Luck★★★★☆
Competition★★★★☆
⏱ Round: 10-15 min
📝 Estimate: ~700-800 LOC
🔧 Difficulty: Medium
Why it fits: Great strategy/luck balance via dice. Both players roll simultaneously so there's less downtime waiting for turns. "YAHTZEE!" moments are genuinely exciting. Dice animations would be fun to build. Risk management decisions every turn.
Key components: 5 dice with roll/hold toggle, 13-category scorecard UI, scoring logic (patterns, straights, full house), simultaneous play sync, running score comparison.

❌⭕ Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe

Strategy Lover's Pick — meta-game mind bender

9 mini tic-tac-toe boards in a 3×3 grid. Where you play in a mini-board determines which mini-board your opponent must play next. Win 3 mini-boards in a row to win the meta-game. The "forced board" mechanic creates deep strategy around sending your opponent to bad positions.

Fun★★★★☆
Strategy★★★★★
Luck★☆☆☆☆
Competition★★★★☆
⏱ Round: 5-8 min
📝 Estimate: ~600-700 LOC
🔧 Difficulty: Medium
⚠ Concern: Very strategy-heavy, almost zero luck element. Great if you both enjoy chess-like thinking, but might frustrate if one player finds the meta first. Draws are possible.
Key components: Nested 3×3×3×3 grid, active-board highlighting, forced-board mechanic, mini-board win detection, meta-board win detection, Socket.IO turn sync.

🃏 War / Speed War

Pure Luck + Speed — casual banter game

Classic War: simultaneous card flip, higher card wins both. Ties trigger "war" (3 face-down, flip 4th). Speed variant: both flip simultaneously, first to slap a matching pair wins the pile. Great for chatting while playing.

Fun★★★☆☆
Strategy★☆☆☆☆
Luck★★★★★
Competition★★★☆☆
⏱ Round: 3-5 min
📝 Estimate: ~400-500 LOC
🔧 Difficulty: Low
⚠ Concern: Too luck-based, may get boring fast. Zero meaningful decisions in classic mode. Better as a "palette cleanser" between strategic games than a standalone addition.
Key components: 52-card deck shuffle, card flip animation, comparison logic, war sequence handling, pile tracking, card art (SVG or CSS).

🎯 Recommendation

Build Battleship first — it has the ideal strategy/luck balance, head-to-head competition, satisfying rounds, and architecturally reuses the Ludo Socket.IO infrastructure (rooms, turns, game state sync). The ship-placement phase adds a unique pre-game layer.

Then Connect Four as a quick add — so simple it could be a weekend project, giving a fast "one more round" option alongside the longer Battleship games.
